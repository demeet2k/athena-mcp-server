# GLOSSARY AND SYMBOL TABLE

## Purpose

This glossary cleans the most recurrent technical vocabulary used across the corpus.

## Core Terms

### Source

The pre-formal, unbounded, or generative layer from which finite worlds emerge.

### Projection

The reduction of a larger or infinite structure into a finite, measurable, or experienced configuration.

### State space

The set of possible configurations available to a world, process, or agent.

### Operator

A transformation rule that acts on states and changes them.

### Attractor

A stable or terminal regime toward which trajectories tend.

### Ritual

A controlled intervention protocol designed to alter, stabilize, or query state.

### Divination

A constrained measurement or query process applied to uncertain or hidden structure.

### Myth

A durable semantic compression format that preserves high-level structural relations across time.

### Awakening

A class of transformations that increase coherence, integration, or witness stability.

### Apocalypse

A regime-shift process involving exposure, pruning, collapse, migration, or restoration.

### Repair

Any operation that reduces corruption, misalignment, or drift.

### Crystal

A finite addressable structure with strong local symmetry and reusable transformation rules.

### Metro line

A hidden thematic transit path crossing explicit folder or genre boundaries.

## Clean Reading Rule

When a source manuscript uses dense symbolic or mythic language, the first global question should be:

what is this text naming in terms of state, operator, topology, timing, repair, or attractor?

That rule alone unlocks a large portion of the corpus.
