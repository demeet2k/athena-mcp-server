# Structural Contracts

The Earth H6 layer does not invent a new topology. It regularizes the topology already compiled by the tesseract v4 bundle.

Chapter contracts are derived from the existing twenty-one chapter stations. Each contract keeps four things explicit:

- the load-bearing role of the chapter inside the Earth layer,
- the legality obligation that must hold before any lift,
- the replay obligation that keeps the chapter rereadable,
- the outbound handoff target, if any, for Fire, Water, or Air.

Appendix contracts are derived from the existing appendix crystal `AppA` through `AppQ`. Earth classifies them into three contract classes only:

- mandatory core: `AppA`, `AppI`, `AppM`
- Earth stability overlays: `AppB`, `AppK`, `AppL`, `AppP`
- conditional lift and transport support: `AppE`, `AppF`, `AppG`, `AppH`, `AppN`, `AppO`, `AppQ`

No appendix is renamed. No appendix topology is rewritten. The Earth layer only states what each appendix is allowed to do for structural stabilization.

The contract families are compressed into four existing Earth laws:

- `E1` Legality Core
- `E2` Law-to-Activation Release
- `E3` Support-to-Flow Banking
- `E4` Proof and Return

Those families are the only sanctioned compression surface for this pass.
