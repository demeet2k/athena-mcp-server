# Third Wave Corpus Synthesis Protocol

## Mission

Perform a deeper-than-summary synthesis of the entire corpus by recursively decomposing it into smaller reading fronts, then contracting the results into larger metro-mapped structures.

## Expansion Law

1. Split corpus by manuscript family.
2. Split each manuscript by chapter.
3. Split each chapter by section.
4. Split each section by atomic node if needed.
5. Assign each front to an elemental or crystal-agent role.

## Contraction Law

1. Section outputs contract into chapter syntheses.
2. Chapter syntheses contract into manuscript syntheses.
3. Manuscript syntheses contract into family syntheses.
4. Family syntheses contract into one emergent metro map and one categorized crystal.

## Required Surfaces

- core metro map
- deeper emergent metro map
- appendix-only control plane
- categorization and synthesis map
- build queue
- validation queue

## Anti-Shallow Rule

Do not stop at “what the corpus says.”
Push toward:

- hidden lines
- shared operator families
- repeated structural laws
- archive-to-runtime transfer opportunities
- missing surfaces whose absence blocks future leverage

## Output Artifact Types

- markdown maps
- atlas refreshes
- registry entries
- receipts
- queue updates
- runtime-bound schemas when justified
