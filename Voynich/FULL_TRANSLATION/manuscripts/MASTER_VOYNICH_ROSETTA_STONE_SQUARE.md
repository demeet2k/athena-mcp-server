## II. Morphological Tiers

### II.1 Prefixes

Prefixes are pre-root operators. They tell the root what is being done to it.

Key classes:

- selective operators: `ot-`, `ok-`
- universal operators: `d-`, `s-`, `o-`, `qo-`, `y-`, `ch-`, `sh-`, `op-`, `of-`, `t-`, `k-`, `p-`, `l-`, `r-`, `f-`, `g-`

Square reading:

- `ot-` = time drives a T-family target
- `ok-` = phase governs a K-family target
- `qo-` = cycle is introduced into the target
- `d-` = fixation is requested
- `s-` = dissolution is requested

### II.2 Roots

Roots are carrier declarations. They name the material, apparatus, substrate, or active principle.

Major families:

- Tria prima roots: `che`, `cho`, `cha`
- matrix roots: `al`, `ol`, `or`, `ar`, `ke`, `te`, `sal`, `sar`
- apparatus roots: `kal`, `kol`, `tal`, `tol`, `cth`, `ckh`, `kch`

Square reading:

- roots are not actions; they are typed state sectors
- the prefix does not create matter; it acts on declared matter

### II.3 Suffixes
