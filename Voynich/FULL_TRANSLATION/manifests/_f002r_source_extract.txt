## 1. Physical Description of the Page

### 1.1 Plant Illustration

**Source:** Wikibooks f2r physical description; Beinecke scan; Stolfi annotations.

**One plant, centered on page.**

**ROOT:**
- Left-right symmetrical, planar
- Anthropomorphic form: "headless stick man with outstretched arms and gripping hands"
- Overpainted with dark color

**STEM:**
- Short and thin
- Two main branches, almost upright, slightly bent
- Irregular thickness, awkward shape
- Light-colored with a **dark vein grossly painted along the middle**

**LEAVES:**
- Dark overpaint
- Shaped like a triangular fan with five lanceolated fingers
- Medium stalks, look like branches

**FLOWERS:**
- **Three flowers** at tips of main branches
- Longish stalks (look like branches)
- Chalyx: oblong oval swelling, partial dark overpaint
- Petals: many, narrow and long — **"the flower looks like a shaving brush"**
- Core: **hidden by petals** (not visible)

| Plant Zone | Feature | VML Process Meaning |

| **ROOT** | Dark overpaint | Loaded/saturated source — material is present and ready. |
| **STEM** | Short and thin | **LOW-VOLUME precision extraction.** Brief, targeted flow — not bulk processing. |
| **STEM** | Dark vein along center | **ESSENCE PATH** — the specific route of concentrated material within the broader channel. |
| **FLOWER** | Three flowers | **THREE products, grades, or completion cycles.** Count = number of outputs or iterations. |
| **FLOWER** | "Shaving brush" (many fine petals) | **DIFFUSE/DISTRIBUTED product.** Final preparation spread across multiple doses or applications. |
| **FLOWER** | Hidden core (obscured by petals) | **SEALED and PROTECTED essence** within the product matrix. Cannot be accessed without breaking through. |

### 1.3 Botanical Identification

| Identifier | Proposed Plant | Basis |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| **Sherwood** | Diffuse Knapweed (*Centaurea diffusa*) | Branching pattern, thistle-like flowers, long taproot |
| **Bax** | *Centaurea* genus (knapweed/cornflower) | Flower/leaf/root, named after Centaur Chiron |
| **Voynich Portal** | *Centaurea* (depicted "fairly accurately") | J.K. Petersen assessment |
| **Wikibooks** | *Centaurea cyanus* (cornflower) — but leaf mismatch noted | Flower shape matches; leaves of C. cyanus are long/thin, unlike f2r's fan-shaped |

**Consensus:** Widely accepted as *Centaurea* genus. Bax proposed the first two words of Paragraph 2 read as "KNT/ə/IR CH/ə/UR" = Centaur Chiron, linking the plant name to the mythological healer. This is one of the more widely discussed proposed plant identifications.

**Under VML framework:** Safe herb, gentle extraction. The reaching root (open posture, no danger markers) contrasts maximally with f1v's claw-root. This is the manuscript's first demonstration of a NON-DANGEROUS procedure.

### 1.4 Layout

Two paragraphs, left- and right-justified: one at top (~6.4 lines), narrower, interrupted by the three flowers; one near bottom (~5.4 lines), interrupted by main stem.

### 3.3 What Makes F2r Unique

**The SAIIN Signature:**

F2r is the **salt-closure page**. The `saiin` marker (salt-seal-cycle-complete) appears 4 times, including the striking `saiin.saiin` doubling in P2.10. This is the highest saiin concentration in the first 8 folios. Compare:

| Folio | saiin count | daiin count | Ratio saiin:daiin |
|-------|------------|------------|-------------------|
| f1r | 0 | 8 | 0:8 |
| f1v | 0 | 1 | 0:1 |
| **f2r** | **4** | **4** | **1:1** |
| f2v | 0 | 3 | 0:3 |

In the VML framework, `saiin` marks salt-phase completion — the crystallization/precipitation step where dissolved material precipitates into solid salt. The **three flowers** directly correspond to three salt-closure cycles plus the doubled saiin as final verification.

**Visual-textual count:** Three flowers = three products. Four saiin markers ≈ three cycles + one doubled verification. This numerical correspondence is specific to f2r.

**The Y-/KY- Presence:**

The moist/receptive prefix family (y-, ky-) accounts for 10.1% of tokens — roughly double the rate on f1v (where danger markers dominate instead). This matches the GENTLE ROOT morphology: reaching hands, open posture, safe feedstock.

**Non-Standard Cycle Markers:**

F2r contains three cycle-marker variants found nowhere else in Quire 1: `daiildy`, `daiirdl`, `daiiny`. These extended forms suggest sub-processes or variant completion states — consistent with a multi-product (three-flower) process that needs to track which cycle produced which fraction.

**The `fodan` Token:**

The `f-` prefix (formative/vegetative) is the rarest operational prefix in VML. Its appearance in `fodan` (f-o-d-an = formative-heat-fix-complete) on this page — which depicts a *Centaurea* (medicinal herb known for wound healing) — is consistent with a GROWTH or REGENERATIVE operation rather than pure extraction.

## 5. What This Page Is

### 5.1 Process Summary

F2r describes a **GENTLE MULTI-CYCLE SALT CRYSTALLIZATION** from a safe medicinal herb (Centaurea). The process has a fundamentally different character from f1v:

| Feature | f1v (Dangerous) | f2r (Safe) |
|---------|----------------|------------|
| Root type | Aggressive (claws) | Gentle (reaching hands) |
| Safety apparatus | Fire seals (CFH), triple valve checks | Standard valve (CKH), no fire seals |
| Primary operation | Reflux + chemical wedding (dam) | Salt crystallization (saiin) |
| Products | ONE sealed product | THREE distributed products |
| Stem | Thick (bulk flow) | Thin (precision) |
| Product character | Dark concentrated core | Hidden core, diffuse petals |

This is not a coincidence — it demonstrates that the manuscript **systematically differentiates procedures** through both visual and textual encoding.

### 5.2 Visual-Textual Redundancy

| Visual Feature | Textual Correspondence | Match Quality |
|---------------|----------------------|---------------|
| Reaching root (safe/gentle) | y-/ky- prefix elevation (10.1%); no CFH fire seals | ✅ STRONG |
| Thin stem (precision) | Low token count (~89 vs f1r's 157); brief procedure | ✅ MODERATE |
| Dark vein in stem (essence path) | sh-e (transition-essence) morphemes trace flow path | ✅ MODERATE |
| Three flowers | Three saiin markers + doubled fourth | ✅ STRONG — numerical match |
| "Shaving brush" petals (distributed product) | Multiple sal/saiin salt-closures = distributed crystallization | ✅ MODERATE |
| Hidden core | keol (quintessence-fluid) = essence locked inside liquid matrix | ✅ MODERATE |

### 5.3 Relationship to Preceding Folios

**F1r → F1v → F2r** forms a three-page introductory sequence:

1. **F1r** (text-only): APPARATUS SPECIFICATION — defines equipment and safety protocols
2. **F1v** (dangerous plant): MAXIMUM DANGER PROCEDURE — demonstrates the hardest case first (poison, reflux, fire seals, chemical wedding)
3. **F2r** (safe plant): STANDARD GENTLE PROCEDURE — demonstrates routine work (safe herb, salt crystallization, multiple products)

This is a **pedagogical progression**: equipment → hardest case → standard case. A master teaching an apprentice would structure the material exactly this way — establish what can kill you, then show how most work actually proceeds.
