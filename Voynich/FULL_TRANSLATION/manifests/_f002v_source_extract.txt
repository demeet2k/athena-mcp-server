## 1. Physical Description of the Page

### 1.1 Plant Illustration

**Source:** Wikibooks f2v physical description; Beinecke scan; J.K. Petersen (Voynich Portal) detailed analysis.

**One plant, centered on page.**

**ROOT:**
- Long straight **horizontal rhizome** (lies flat, extends sideways)
- Covered with **scales or round stalk scars** — sequential markings along the length
- Rhizome is the primary root structure (no taproot)

**STEM:**
- Single, vertical, slightly bent
- Shoots from the middle of the rhizome
- **Dark-painted** (loaded/saturated)
- Stub of a lost branch at mid-stalk

**LEAVES:**
- **Single large leaf**, horizontal
- Shaped like a very fat C (reniform/kidney-shaped) with smooth rim
- Painted solid dark
- Short stalk

**FLOWER:**
- **One white flower**, pointing almost straight up
- Long, light-colored stalk with darker vein; partially obscured by leaf
- Chalyx: well-drawn, thick tongue-shaped sepals, light-colored
- Petals: well-drawn, flaring, **lily-shaped**, serrated edges, white underpaint
- Core: **not visible**
- Stamen: long, with a few short hairs

| **LEAF** | Single, large, reniform/kidney | **BROAD SURFACE AREA for evaporation/absorption.** The kidney shape suggests a collection basin or condensation plate. |

| **FLOWER** | Serrated petal edges | **FINE-GRAINED product** — the edges are worked, not smooth. Indicates refined/processed material. |
| **FLOWER** | Invisible core, long stamen | Product essence is **DIFFUSED through the structure** rather than concentrated in a core. Long stamen = extended reach of the active principle. |
| **FLOWER** | Light stalk with dark vein | The **ESSENCE TRAVELS THROUGH** the supporting structure as a dark concentrated thread. |

### 1.3 Botanical Identification

| Identifier | Proposed Plant | Basis |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| **Ethel Voynich (ELV)** | Water lily (*Nymphaea*) | Kidney leaf, aquatic rhizome |
| **Zandbergen** | *Nymphaea candida* / *N. alba* | Water lily; leaf shape |
| **Sherwood** | *Nymphoides* | Genus-level only (2008) |
| **J.K. Petersen** | *Nymphoides* / *Villarsia* (floating hearts) | Flower shape mismatch with Nymphaea; fringed petals, visible calyx ridge, long pistil — all match Nymphoides better |
| **Scott** | *Nymphoides aquatica* | North American species |

**Debate:** The kidney-shaped leaf and horizontal rhizome point to an aquatic plant. However, J.K. Petersen demonstrated that the **flower** does not match *Nymphaea* — it has features (calyx, long pistil, fringed petals) more consistent with *Nymphoides* (floating hearts), a related but distinct genus. The rhizome's sequential scars match aquatic plants that spread through lateral growth.

**Under VML framework:** The aquatic identity is consistent with the page's function as a water-based volatile purification procedure. The specific species matters less than the process it encodes: sequential horizontal processing through a wet medium yielding a purified white product.

### 1.4 Layout

Two paragraphs, left- and right-justified: one at the top (interrupted by the single flower), one just below mid-page (interrupted by the main stem). Text wraps around the plant.

## 2. Cross-Validated EVA Transcription

### Paragraph 1 (P1) — Upper text block

```
P1.1: kooiin.cheopchor.otaiin.odain.chor.dair.shty-
P1.2: kchokchy.sho.shol.qotcho.loeees.qoty.chor.daiin-
P1.3: otchy.chor.lshy.chol.chody.chodain.chcthy.daiin-
P1.4: sho.cholo.cheor.chodaiin=
```

**Transcription notes:**
- P1.1: `kooiin` — first word. K-oo-iin = contain-[double heat]-cycle. Double-heated containment.
- P1.1: `cheopchor` — complex compound: che-op-chor = volatile-essence-press-volatile-heat-outlet. Compressed volatile essence flowing to outlet.
- P1.2: `loeees` — l-o-eee-s = LOCK-heat-[triple essence]-dissolve. Triple-intensified essence dissolved — extremely rare triple-e marker.
- P1.2: `qotcho` — qo-tch-o = circulate-driven-heat. Retort under driven heat.
- P1.4: `chodaiin` — ch-o-d-aiin = volatile-heat-fix-[cycle-complete]. Terminal: volatile heated and fixed through complete cycle.

### Paragraph 2 (P2) — Lower text block

```
P2.5: kchor.shy.daiiin.chckhy.sshey.dor.chol.daiin-
P2.6: dor.chol.chor.chol.keol.chy.chty.daiin.otchor.chan-
P2.7: daiin.chotchey.qoteeey.chokeos.chees.chr.cheaiin-
P2.8: chokoishe.chor.chiol.chol.dolody=
```

**Transcription notes:**
- P2.5: `daiiin` — TRIPLE-i variant. Extended cycle: d-aiii-n = fix-[triple iteration]-complete. THREE full cycles rather than the standard two. Only occurrence in Quire 1 outside of f1r.
- P2.6: `dor.chol.chor.chol` — alternating d-or / ch-ol pattern. Fix-outlet / volatile-fluid / volatile-heat-outlet / volatile-fluid. This is a **washing sequence**: fix, release volatile fluid, collect at outlet, release volatile fluid again.
- P2.6: `keol` — ke-ol = QUINTESSENCE-fluid. The purified active principle in liquid. Same token as f2r.
- P2.7: `qoteeey` — qo-teee-y = circulate-[triple driven essence]-active. Triple-essence retort cycling.
- P2.7: `chokeos` — ch-ok-e-o-s = volatile-phase-essence-heat-dissolve. Volatile phase-shifted essence heated then dissolved.
- P2.8: `dolody` — d-ol-o-dy = fix-fluid-heat-fixed. TERMINAL: the fluid is heated and permanently fixed. Final word of the page.

### 3.3 What Makes F2v Unique

**The CH- Saturation:**

F2v is the **VOLATILE SPIRIT page**. Over half its tokens (54.9%) begin with ch-, a concentration unmatched anywhere in the early herbals. This is not gradual — it's a step function:

| Folio | ch- % | Character |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| f1r | 18.5% | Apparatus specification |
| f1v | 26.5% | Dangerous extraction |
| f2r | 18.0% | Salt crystallization |
| **f2v** | **54.9%** | **PURE VOLATILE PROCESSING** |

The page is SATURATED with volatile operations, corresponding to the dark-painted stem and leaf (saturated with extract) and the white product flower (purified volatile). The text does almost nothing but manipulate volatile spirits.

**Zero Salt (SAIIN = 0):**

F2v contains no salt operations whatsoever. Compare f2r's 4 saiin markers. These are complementary pages: f2r is the salt-closure page (saiin-heavy, ch-moderate), f2v is the volatile-purification page (ch-saturated, saiin-zero). They sit on opposite sides of the same leaf, encoding opposite operations.

**The Triple Markers:**

F2v contains both `daiiin` (triple-cycle verification) and `qoteeey` (triple-essence retort cycling). These triple-intensity markers are extremely rare across the corpus. Together with the `loeees` (triple-essence dissolve) in P1.2, f2v has **three separate triple-intensity tokens** — more than any other folio in Quire 1. This is a HIGH-POWER volatile purification: the essences are intensified, the cycling is extended, and the verification is tripled.

**The Washing Sequence (P2.6):**

`dor.chol.chor.chol` is a four-token alternating pattern: fix-outlet / volatile-fluid / volatile-outlet / volatile-fluid. This textual pattern directly encodes WASHING — the repeated rinsing of material with volatile solvent, collecting at the outlet, and repeating. It corresponds to the sequential scars on the horizontal rhizome (each scar = one wash cycle).

## 5. What This Page Is

### 5.1 Process Summary

F2v describes **VOLATILE SPIRIT PURIFICATION THROUGH REPEATED WASHING** — the process of extracting, concentrating, and purifying a volatile essence from an aquatic plant using iterative washing cycles and triple-intensity retort cycling. The product is a WHITE PURIFIED VOLATILE — an albedo-stage preparation.

Three-phase structure:
1. **DRIVEN EXTRACTION** (P1.1–P1.2): Volatile compressed and driven by heat through the retort. Triple-essence captured at the lock point (`loeees`).
2. **WASHING LOOP** (P2.5–P2.6): Iterative outlet-fluid-outlet-fluid cycling purifies the volatile. Three complete cycles verified (`daiiin`). Quintessence appears in liquid form (`keol`).
3. **TRIPLE RETORT AND FINAL LOCK** (P2.7–P2.8): The purified volatile is retorted at triple intensity (`qoteeey`), then permanently fixed as a fluid (`dolody`).

### 5.2 Relationship to Adjacent Folios

**F2r (recto) vs F2v (verso) — same physical leaf, complementary operations:**

| Feature | f2r (Salt) | f2v (Volatile) |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| Dominant prefix | s-/saiin (11.2%) | ch- (54.9%) |
| Salt markers (saiin) | 4 | 0 |
| Product type | Three solid salt-crystal products | One white purified volatile liquid |
| Visual flower count | Three (multiple products) | One (single product) |
| Visual root | Reaching hands (gentle, open) | Horizontal rhizome (sequential, iterative) |
| Process character | Crystallization/precipitation | Washing/distillation |

These two pages form a **SALT-MERCURY PAIR** — the two fundamental principles of spagyric pharmacy separated across two sides of the same leaf. F2r captures the fixed (salt/body). F2v captures the volatile (mercury/spirit). In alchemical philosophy, these two operations are always complementary.
