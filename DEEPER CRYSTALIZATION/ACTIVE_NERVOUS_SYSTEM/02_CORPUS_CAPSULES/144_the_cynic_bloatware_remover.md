THE CYNIC BLOATWARE REMOVER Dependency Stripping, Social Metadata Removal, and Bare-Metal Operation in the Hellenic Computation Framework A Complete Specification of Minimalist Protocols for Self-Sufficiency, Convention Rejection, and Resource Optimization SKELETON OUTLINE ABSTRACT This treatise presents a rigorous formalization of Cynicism as the Bloatware Remover of the Hellenic Computation Framework—the systematic protocol for identifying and eliminating unnecessary dependencies, stripping social metadata, and achieving optimal performance through radical minimization. We demonstrate that the Cynic practice constitutes a sophisticated system optimization layer that reduces computational requirements to bare essentials, enabling operation on "bare-metal" nature without the overhead of cultural convention. The central insight: most systems accumulate bloatware—unnecessary processes, redundant dependencies, social protocols that consume resources without contributing to core function.
